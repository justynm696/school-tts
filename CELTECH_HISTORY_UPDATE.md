# CELTECH History Update Summary

## Overview
Successfully integrated accurate historical information about Central Luzon College of Science and Technology (CELTECH College) into the V.I.R.A. application with full text-to-speech support.

## Changes Made

### Updated History Section in `data.js`
Replaced the previous generic history with **6 comprehensive entries** covering:

1. **Founding and Early History (1959)**
   - Conceptualized by Doña Helen P. Legaspi on May 20, 1959
   - Officially opened September 24, 1959, in San Fernando, Pampanga
   - Started as Artistic Vocational School (AVS)
   - Focus on fashion and vocational education

2. **Expansion to Olongapo (1970s-1980s)**
   - Established presence in Olongapo City
   - Became education hub for Amerasians
   - Served children of U.S. servicemen from nearby naval bases

3. **Evolution into CELTECH**
   - Transformed into "model institution of higher learning"
   - Adopted current name to reflect broader academic offerings
   - Main campus in former Olongapo Doctor's Hospital, Upper Kalaklan
   - Modern facilities: labs, mini-hospital, mini-hotel, 200-seat library

4. **Current Leadership and Recent Milestones**
   - Leadership: Dr. Renato P. Legaspi (President/CEO), Dr. Rene Paulo Legaspi (SVP/COO)
   - Celebrated 66th Founding Anniversary (2025/2026)
   - Specialized programs: Criminology, Maritime
   - Digital services: Dragonpay, AIMS Student Information System

5. **Mission and Vision**
   - Leading career development center
   - High-quality education in science, technology, culture, arts
   - Producing competent, innovative, socially responsible professionals
   - Credo: **"We Teach. We Train. We Touch. We Transform."**

6. **Student Resources and Services**
   - AIMS Student Account for grades, schedules, messages
   - Online banking via Dragonpay (e-wallets, mobile banking)
   - Financial assistance: TES, TDP
   - NaMMAT registration for maritime students
   - Contact: +63 917 114 0297, info@clcst.com.ph

## How to Access

3. **Listen to Content**: Click on any history card to hear it read aloud
